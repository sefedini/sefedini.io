Kosovo has several types of traditional dances:


Vallja e Shotes

One of the most popular dances in Kosovo. The dance is played in several variants: two women or girls, a woman and a man (or even two couples), two men, etc.
It is a lyrical dance. It is played at all festive gatherings and more during the wedding celebration. It is accompanied by various vocal-instrumental forms.

Vallja e Rugoves

The Rugova dance is mainly a dance for men. It originated from the villages of Rugova, but nowadays it is used in many provinces. The musical instrument that conveys the rhythm of this dance, among the most important, is the tupani.

Vallja e Tropojes

The dance is traditionally danced in the Highlands of Gjakova. The folk dances of the Highlands of Gjakova stand out for their special rhythm and expressiveness.
The distinguishing feature of men's folk dances is strength and dynamism, although they are characterized by spontaneity in interpretation, they also have a specific theme.
While the women's dances are very dynamic, elegant, sometimes explosive, but also laid out with pride and grace. Imagine the flight of Albanians to Sokol, there starts the rhythm of the mountain dance of the Gjakova Highlands.
